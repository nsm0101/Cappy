# Sanction Policy

> **Status:** Stub.

**Owner:** Founder
**Implements:** 45 CFR §164.308(a)(1)(ii)(C)
**Last reviewed:** [DATE]

## Purpose

Document the consequences of workforce members failing to comply
with security policies and procedures.

## Scope

Applies to all workforce members, including the founder, any future
employees, and any contractors with access to PHI.

## Sanction levels

| Severity | Example | Sanction |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Minor (first time) | Failure to follow a documented procedure with no PHI exposure | Documented coaching; refresher training |
| Minor (repeated) | Same as above, after coaching | Written warning; mandatory retraining |
| Moderate | Negligent handling that risks PHI exposure (e.g., emailing PHI to wrong address with no real-world impact) | Written warning; access review; possible suspension of access |
| Severe | Intentional unauthorized access; willful policy violation; refusal to follow security procedures | Termination; reporting to authorities if criminal |
| Catastrophic | Intentional disclosure of PHI; theft of data; deliberate sabotage | Immediate termination; civil and criminal referral |

## Founder accountability

The founder is subject to the same sanctions. In a single-founder
context this is enforced by:
- The Compliance & Documentation agent flagging policy violations in
  retros
- The founder publicly logging any sanction-level events in the risk
  assessment
- The board (when one exists) having visibility into any severe
  events

## Process

1. Suspected violation triggers an investigation (incident-response
   procedure if PHI is involved)
2. Findings documented
3. Sanction determined per the table above
4. Sanction recorded in the workforce member's access record
5. Affected user(s) notified per breach notification policy if
   applicable

## No retaliation

Workforce members reporting suspected violations in good faith are
protected from retaliation. This protection is documented and
explicit.
